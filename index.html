Featured Work

Projects

1

Low-cost 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi radar with signal attenuation capability

Development of a low-cost prototype designed to detect Wi-Fi signals using an ESP32 microprocessor and a modified antenna configured as a directional antenna. The system captures Wi-Fi signals and estimates their position and distance through RSSI measurements. It also includes an option to select a previously scanned signal and attenuate it using a voltage-controlled oscillator, generating noise at the operating frequencies of 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi channels.

2

Autonomous vehicle controlled by a color selector

Development of an autonomous vehicle equipped with a calibrated color sensor capable of triggering three actions: moving forward, turning left, and turning right. Color selection could be performed by placing colored blocks directly in front of the sensor or by controlling the selector through Bluetooth.
